A Year After Leaving Corporate:What I Built in the Quiet

Event & Experiential Strategy
The Devil Wears Prada 2 opens this Friday. Twenty years later, the standard still holds. The sequel isn't just on screen.
By Antroinette Smith April 30, 2026 5 min read

I. The Hook
The Sequel That Wrote Itself
When the first Devil Wears Prada dropped in 2006, it didn't just entertain — it codified something. Excellence isn't aspirational. It's operational. Miranda Priestly didn't hope for precision. She required it.
This Friday, the sequel arrives and flips the premise: what happens after you've spent years mastering someone else's world — and you decide to build your own?
"She never announced. She arrived."
That question landed differently for me. Because exactly one year ago, I answered it.
II. The Parallel
Walking Away from the Room You Built In
May 9, 2025. My last day at Alcon Vision. No dramatic exit. No countdown post. Just a thoughtful email to the people who mattered — and a quiet decision that what I'd spent 15+ years perfecting — the architecture behind enterprise events, brand activations, and large-scale experiences — deserved its own stage.
So I built one.
"Miranda Priestly would never announce. She'd arrive. So consider this our arrival."
I carried everything that mattered: the instinct for cross-functional alignment, the muscle memory of managing multimillion-dollar program budgets, the discipline of keeping 47 stakeholders synchronized without a single one feeling managed. I left behind the part where someone else's name was on the door.

III. The Reveal
What The Artfullyours Agency Is
A boutique white-label event and experiential consultancy, partnering with enterprise brands, agencies, and organizations to design and execute high-impact experiences with discretion, precision, and creative excellence. We sit behind the curtain of some of the most high-touch brand moments in the room — and we like it there.
From comprehensive event strategy and program design to immersive brand activations and white-label production — we serve as the strategic backbone behind enterprise event experiences. End-to-end management of complex programs where stakeholder alignment and creative vision are never compromised. The timeline holds. The room moves. And no one ever sees the scaffolding.
"We design moments people carry with them. The kind of evidence no brief can capture — you had to be there."
The details aren't just details.
They make the design.

IV. The Standard
The Details Make the Design
In event strategy, there's no post-production. No second take. The room is live. The stakeholders are watching. The timeline either held or it didn't. That pressure is where I've always operated best — not because I thrive on chaos, but because I've spent my career building the systems that prevent it.
Scope definition. Risk mitigation. Vendor alignment. Budget architecture. Cross-functional communication that anticipates the question before it's asked. These aren't line items. They're the infrastructure that lets the creative vision actually land.
Twenty years after Miranda Priestly set the standard, the lesson still holds: the details aren't just details. They make the experience. They make the brand. And if you're doing it right, no one ever sees the scaffolding.
V. The Invitation
More to Come This Summer
Hard launch coming this summer. Website. Case studies. The full portfolio of what we've been building behind the scenes. For now, this is our arrival — artfully.
If your brand needs someone who treats your event like their own reputation is on the line — because it is — we should talk.
"You build your own. Artfully."
